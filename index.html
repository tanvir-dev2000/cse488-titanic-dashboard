Key Insights
01
Women & Children First
Gender is the single most powerful predictor at 48.1% feature importance. Female passengers had nearly 4× the survival rate of males, confirming evacuation priority protocols.
02
Class Privilege Persisted
Pclass and Fare together account for ~29% of feature importance. 1st class passengers survived at 2.6× the rate of 3rd class, reflecting both deck access and evacuation priority.
03
Sweet Spot for Families
Passengers traveling with 1–3 family members had the highest survival rates (55–72%). Solo travelers and very large families fared poorly — small families could navigate together efficiently.
04
Random Forest Wins
Random Forest achieved AUC 0.892 vs Logistic Regression's 0.849 and Decision Tree's 0.624. Ensemble methods capture the complex nonlinear interactions between class, gender and fare.
05
Cherbourg Advantage
Passengers boarding at Cherbourg (France) had a 55.4% survival rate, significantly higher than Southampton's 33.9%. Cherbourg embarked predominantly 1st class passengers.
06
Youth Survival Premium
Children under 10 had the highest survival rate at 61.3%, reflecting prioritization during evacuation. The 60+ age group had the lowest survival at 26.9%, likely due to mobility constraints.
